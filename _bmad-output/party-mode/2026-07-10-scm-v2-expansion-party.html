One word, three physics

📊 Mary: The most dangerous word in this document is "issue". Three physical realities hid under one verb — consumed, accumulated into a prototype, loaned — and if anyone collapses them back, a Rs 8 lakh oscilloscope leaves the ledger as a consumable and nobody can answer "who has it".
🏗️ Winston: And your build record needs a foreign key, not a paragraph. If build 7 swapped a 10k resistor for two 4k7 in series, the record of truth is the as-built snapshot line — "changed some resistors" in a lab notebook is a feature without an artifact.

The lathe has one parts list

🏗️ Winston: Machine spare-parts lists are equipment BOMs. I claim the structure record, you keep the schedule — otherwise two lists for the same lathe disagree by the next audit.
🦺 Ravi: Fine — spares where-used hangs off your structures, because I refuse to keep a second parts list that goes stale by Diwali. But remember half my technicians are hats, not badges: the maker-hub kid on the front desk is my first responder on Sundays.

The statute always wins

🧮 Meera: FR-I-05 as written ships LIFO as a configuration option, and Ind AS 2 permits FIFO and weighted average only. Strike it today or the first statutory audit strikes it for you, with a qualification attached.
♻️ Farida: Which is why I hard-code nothing — the 1961 Act died this April and 206C is now 394. My screens capture fields; the rates live in her lane. Also: tare weighment with six labourers in the truck bed, gross with two. That trick was 400 kilos of copper per lift. Random re-weighment is not negotiable.
🧮 Meera: I once audited a makerspace that booked member materials as "miscellaneous income" — three weekends rebuilding B2C supplies from till rolls before the GST officer did it for us. The hub counter issues a proper invoice or it does not sell.
